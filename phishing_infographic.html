What is Phishing?

How hackers trick you into giving up your info — and how to stop them.

The Basics

Phishing is when a hacker pretends to be someone you trust — like your bank or Amazon — to steal your passwords or personal info. They "fish" for victims by sending fake emails, texts, or calls.

🗂️ Common Types
📧
Email Phishing
Fake emails pretending to be Netflix, PayPal, or your bank.
💬
Smishing (Texts)
"Your package is delayed — click here." Fake texts with bad links.
📞
Vishing (Calls)
Fake calls from "the IRS" or "tech support" trying to scare you.
🎯
Spear Phishing
Targeted attack using your name and details. Harder to spot.
🚩 Red Flags to Watch For
😱
Creates panic — "Act NOW or your account gets deleted!"
🔗
Weird link — like "amaz0n-login.net" instead of amazon.com
🤷
Says "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name
🔑
Asks for your password — no real company ever does this
✅ What To Do
✓
Slow down before clicking Ask yourself: Was I expecting this? Take 5 seconds.
✓
Go directly to the website Type the real URL yourself instead of clicking their link.
✓
Turn on two-factor authentication Even if they get your password, 2FA keeps them out.
✕
Never share your password Legit companies will never ask for it over email or text.
